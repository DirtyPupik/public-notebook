Shifts are generally prefered over mul/div when the operand is a power of 2.

shr - Shift Arithmetic Right (works the same as left).
  • Can be used with C's >> (when operands are signed).
  • First operand (source and destination) is r/mX.
  • Second operand is either cl(lowest byte of rcx) or 1 byte immidiate.
  • Devides by 2.
  • Bits fallen of the right go into the carry flag (CF, CY in MSVS), if it is either 0 or 1.
  • The new bits that are added in arithmitic shift are either one's or zero's, depending on the sign bit.
  • 1111`0000 (-16) >> 1111`1000 (-8)


shl - Shift Arithmetic Left (same as logical left).
  • Can be used with C's << (when operands are signed).
  • First operand (source and destination) is r/mX.
  • Second operand is either cl(lowest byte of rcx) or 1 byte immidiate.
  • Multiplies by 2.
  • Bits fallen of the right go into the carry flag (CF, CY in MSVS), if it is either 0 or 1.
  • The new bits that are added in left arithmethic shift are zero's.
